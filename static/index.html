Methodology

Power-2 limb-darkening coefficients are obtained by trilinear interpolation of published tables in Teff, log(g), and [M/H]. For Johnson–Cousins, Sloan/SDSS, Strömgren, Gaia, Kepler, and TESS filters, coefficients are from Claret & Southworth (2022, A&A 664, A128) using ATLAS9 plane-parallel models across all tabulated metallicities, and from Claret & Southworth (2023, A&A 674, A63) using PHOENIX-COND spherical models at solar metallicity. For the CHEOPS filter, coefficients are from Claret & Southworth (2023) using PHOENIX-COND models (solar metallicity only). For the CBB (Blue Blocking Exoplanet) filter, coefficients are from a power-2 table derived specifically for this filter using ATLAS9 atmosphere models. The PHOENIX-COND coefficients use the M2 (midpoint) truncation method.

The power-2 limb-darkening law is I(μ)/I(1) = 1 − g(1 − μh), where μ = cos θ is the cosine of the angle between the line of sight and the stellar surface normal. Microturbulent velocity is fixed at ξ = 2.0 km/s, the standard value for transit work. The least-squares fitting method is used throughout. No extrapolation is performed beyond the grid boundaries.
